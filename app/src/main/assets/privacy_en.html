Data Protection Information – “Stopp Corona” App

The “Stopp Corona” App (in the following text we only call it the "app") is provided by the Austrian Red Cross:

Whenever you read "we" or "us" in the rest of the document, it means the Austrian Red Cross.

According to the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), we are the controller.

The GDPR is an EU regulation that ensures that your personal data is protected. It is very important to us to protect you and your data. Therefore, we are offering you this app and comply with the requirements of the GDPR and the Austrian Data Protection Act (Datenschutzgesetz, DSG).

In this data protection information we inform you about which of your personal data we process, the purposes for processing as well as the legal basis for it and which rights you have as a data subject if you use this app.

 

2.     What is the purpose of this app?

The main function of this app is to enable you to record contacts with other people, so 

  • you can inform your contacts if you are ill or suspect to be sick
  • you will be informed if one of your contacts has reported sick
  • you will receive an all-clear notice if one of your contacts revokes his or her sick report

 

The app is designed to enable people at risk of infection to take special protective measures and to prevent the virus from spreading, as far as possible.

 

You will also find links in the app that lead to information about the COVID-19 virus and contact points that you can contact for help. There is also a questionnaire where you can enter any symptoms you may have and get an answer as to whether these symptoms indicate that you are infected with the COVID-19 disease. Within this app no decisions solely based on automated processing are made.

 

We expressly point out that the functions and information provided cannot replace the advice of a doctor. In case of any questions about the disease and therapy, we recommend contacting a doctor. We do not make any therapy recommendations when using the app.

 

In order to give you even more details about how the app works, we have set up a page for you where we offer answers to frequently asked questions (FAQ):  https://www.stopp-corona.at/faq_stopp_corona_app/

 

3.     What is personal data?

Personal data includes any information which relates to an identified or identifiable natural person. During the development of this app, we placed great emphasis on processing as little personal data as possible from you. Because: the less data we process from you, the higher the protection of your privacy and the lower the risk of a data breach.

When first using the app, it is not necessary to enter any personal data. Within the operating system of your device a unique, randomly set chains of numbers (“random IDs”) will be generated that the app will use and send to our server in case you report sick.

Specifically, two random-IDs are created:

  1. TEK (= temporary exposure key) – one per day
  2. RPI (=rolling proximity identifier) – this is derived from the TEK and regularly changed in short intervals

By using Huawei Mobile Services, they are:

  1. PK (= periodic key) – one per day
  2. DSC (=dynamic sharing code) – this is derived from the PK and regularly changed in short intervals

When reporting an infection or suspicion thereof, your TEK/PK will become a so-called pseudonymous data. This means, that we cannot identify you with it. But an identification cannot be ruled out completely. The faint possibility remains, that contacts of a person who has reported sick can remember all the contacts in the past few days and will be able to make the connection on who the reporting person is. According to the strict case law (s. ECJ case Breyer) all data will be treated as personal. As provider of the app we cannot identify any users and their contacts though.

 

In addition to "normal" personal data, there is also the colloquially called "sensitive" data. According to the GDPR, these are called "special categories of personal data" (see Art. 9 GDPR). Health data is a type of such sensitive data. These must be handled with even more caution.

In the following sections we inform you about what data we process within the functions of the app and what the legal basis for doing so is.

 

4.   Which personal data of yours are processed?

Depending on your specific use of the functions of the app, we process the following data:

4.1 Data collected when the app is downloaded

If you download the app from the app store (e.g. Google Play Store, Apple App Store and Huawei AppGallery), you will have to enter data there (e.g. user name, email address). This will then be transmitted to the app store and processed along with other information such as the customer number of your account, the time of the download and the individual device code. This data is processed exclusively by the respective app store and is beyond our control and we are not the controller for this processing. Data protection information can be found directly at Google, Apple and Huawei: https://policies.google.com/privacy, https://support.apple.com/de-at/HT210584 or https://consumer.huawei.com/en/privacy/privacy-policy/.

 

4.2 Data that is processed after the app is installed

As already mentioned above, you do not have to enter any personal data to use the app. The app is built to technically not enable identification of users, locations or devices. Solely random IDs are generated and exchanged for recording a contact. Specifically, there are two different random IDs that are generated within the operating system of your device:

 

  1. TEK (= temporary exposure key) – one per day
  2. RPI (= rolling proximity identifier) – this is derived from the TEK and generated new in short intervals (approx. every 10 Minutes)

By using Huawei Mobile Services, they are:

  1. PK (= periodic key) – one per day
  2. DSC (=dynamic sharing code) – this is derived from the PK and regularly changed in short intervals (approx. every 10 Minutes)

 

This data is generated and processed on the level of the operating system of the device as part of the so-called “Exposure Notification API” or “Contact Shield” (s. chapter 5.3.2). We are only in control of this processing, as we use the interface of the provider. Your legal relationship with the provider of your operating system (Android or Apple) is based on their terms and conditions.

 

The app will create one security code per TEK/PK, that will be stored on the device for 14 days. When sending an infection report, the security code will be sent to the server to authenticate the report.

 

The processing of your personal data within the app is based on your explicit consent (Art. 6 Para. 1 lit. a and Art. 9 Para. 2 lit. a GDPR). You can only use the app if you give this consent, which you will be asked to start with. If you change your mind, you can withdraw your consent at any time. As long as you haven't sent a sick report, you can easily do so by uninstalling or deleting the Stopp Corona app. This means that we no longer have any personal data from you. Additionally, you can partially revoke consent by deactivating the automated “digital handshake”. This does not affect the lawfulness of processing until revocation.

 

 

4.3 Data required for the technical provision of the app

Every communication via the Internet requires certain data to enable the flow of information. In this specific case, the following data is required for the installation and use of the app for the communication between your end device (mobile phone) and our server:

 

 - IP address

- Date and time of the request

- Configuration (language settings, device type and version of the operating system)

 

The processing of this personal data of yours is based on the legitimate interest (Article 6 Para 1 lit.f of GDPR): the temporary processing of the data provided by the system is necessary to allow communication between end device and server. A storage or integration of this data with other personal data of yours does not take place.

 

The data will be deleted as soon as they are no longer required for the purpose of their collection. This is the case with the data collected for the purpose of provision of the app when the respective session has ended.

 

4.4 Data that are processed in the digital handshake

The RPI (=rolling proximity identifier) / DSC (=dynamic sharing code) are constantly changing contact number (random ID) of your app that will be transmitted via Bluetooth when in contact with another device that has the activated the app. The RPI will be stored in the device of the contact-person. Due to the transmission via Bluetooth the RPI is detectable in the surrounding. The RPI changes together with the MAC address of BLE (Bluetooth Low Energy) stack approximately every 10 minutes. It will be used for the exchange with other app users. Due to the continuously changing random IDs, it is impossible to track the location of an app user throughout the day. Therefore, the risk is no higher as when using the Bluetooth-function of the device for any other functionality. The DSC (= dynamic sharing code) works in same manner as RPI.

 

If an app user reports an infection or suspicion thereof, the people he or she was in contact with within the past few days should be warned. The specific timeframe for warning is based on epidemiological considerations and can reach up to 14 days. Currently (June 2020), the contacts of the current day including the past 2 days will be informed. The app will send the TEKs of these days to our server. From there every app user will collect the total TEK list once per day and derive the RPIs. By comparing the derived RPIs with the RPIs stored in the device you will be able to determine, whether you were in contact with the person that has reported sick. Stopp Corona App maps PK to TEK to gurantee compatibility and same behaviour.

 

Additionally, encrypted metadata (associated encrypted metadata - AEM) will be processed for determining of the protocol-version and the transmission power for a better approximation of distance measuring. Like the Rolling Proximity Identifier (RPI) or Dynamic Sharing Code (DSC), the encrypted metadata will change in the same interval of approximately 10 minutes to not allow for a wireless tracking of devices throughout the day.

 

The legal basis in both cases is your consent (Art. 6 Para. 1 a GDPR and Art. 9 Para. 2 a GDPR). You can only use this function if you give your consent.

4.5 Data that are processed in the event of reporting sick

There are multiple ways to report sick:

 

  • if you have a medical certificate and your illness is thus definitely established
  • if you suspect to have contracted Covid-19 based on your symptoms
  • if you use the symptom checker (= questionnaire) and based on the results think that you are probably sick

 

Immediately after your report, all persons with whom you have made a digital handshake via the app within the past days (s. chapter 4.4) will be informed.

 

In both cases, you must provide your phone number before submitting the report.

There are several reasons for this:

  1. We want to make sure that there is no abuse. To ensure that no arbitrary number is entered, a TAN will be sent to your cell phone, which you must enter in the registration form to continue.
  2. We can also contact you on the phone number for any assistance you may need.

 

Immediately after your notification of illness, your contacts of the past days (s. chapter 4.4) will be informed that one of your “handshake” contacts is considered infected including the time, when the contact took place. This gives your contacts the opportunity to warn other people with whom they have been in contact with after this time. Your telephone number will never be disclosed to your contacts.

 

The transmission of your sickness report and your telephone number is voluntary. This data processing is justified by your consent (Art. 6 (1) (a) GDPR and Art. 9 (2) (a) GDPR). However, you can only use the sick notification function - and thus the notification of your contacts - if you give your consent. You can revoke your consent at any time, whereby the revocation does not affect the legality of the processing until revoked. Please be aware, that we will not delete your telephone number or sick notice immediately after revoking your consent, but will store it based on a different legal basis: We will then keep the telephone number for 30 days as a measure to be able to follow up, in case the specific suspicion of improper and / or unlawful use arises. This is a necessary processing to assert, exercise or defend legal claims, which is justified by Art. 6 Para. 1 lit. f and Art. 9 Para. 2 lit. f GDPR.

 

 

4.6 Data that will be processed in the event of an all-clear notification

If you sent a warning message to your contacts, after using the Symptom Checker, and it turns out that it fortunately was a "false alarm", because for example a COVID 19 test showed that you are not infected, then you can use the "all-clear" function. This will lead to your previously alarmed handshake partners being informed about this status update.

 

The legal basis for this processing is again your consent (Art. 6 Para. 1 lit. a and Art. 9 Para. 2 lit. a GDPR) that you gave us when installing the app. You are entitled to revoke your consent at any time, whereby the revocation does not affect the legality of the processing until revoked.

If you do not give your consent, this function cannot be used, and your contacts will not receive notification of the all-clear.

 

4.7 Data processed when a sick report is revoked

If you have sent a sick message to your contacts, but this was a "false alarm" (=an erroneous message), then you can use the function "revocation of sick message". As a result, the contacts you have previously notified, will receive a message with the revocation of your sick report.

 

For this you must provide your phone number before submitting the message. With this we want to prevent abuse. To validate your phone number, a TAN will be sent to your mobile phone, which you will need to enter into the registration form to continue.

 

The TAN and the phone number are transmitted via our SMS gateway and then to the server. Your phone number is stored only on the World-Direct server (see chapter 5.3.1.). Your medical certificate or the results of the symptom checker will not be transmitted.

 

The legal basis for this processing is consent (Art.  6 abs.  1 lit. a and Art.  9 sec. 2 lit.  a GDPR) that you gave us when you installed the app. You can revoke this consent at any time, whereby the revocation does not affect the legality of the processing until the revocation.

 

Please note, however, that despite a revocation, we do not immediately delete your telephone number and the revocation of the sick report, as we rely on a different legal basis for further processing: We will keep the telephone number for 30 days if suspicion of abusive and/or unlawful use arises. This is a necessary processing for the assertion, exercise or defense of legal claims, which are justified by Article 6 (1) lit. f and Art. f GDPR.

 

If you do not allow the revocation of the sick message, this function cannot be used, and your contacts will not receive a notification of the revocation.

 

4.8 Data processed in the course of recommending the app

If you recommend the app to your friends or acquaintances, this is done through the respective sharing function of your operating system. In the course of submitting a recommendation, you select an application that will release the link to the Stopp Corona app (E-Mail, Signal, Whatsapp, etc.). This processing is carried out exclusively by you and is outside our sphere of influence, which is why we are not the controller for this processing.

 

4.9 Data that we process for statistical purposes

As of the Stopp Corona app version 1.1.3. (21st of April 2020) we will not process data for statistical purposes. In further versions though, we will count the number of messages and handshakes and the number of installations of the app. With whom the contacts took place is not recorded.

This quantitative data is processed without the possibility of traceability to your person, thus in an anonymized and aggregated form, for statistical purposes. The legal basis for this is Section 7 (1) 2 DSG in conjunction with Art. 6 (1) lit. a and Art. 9 Para. 2 lit. a GDPR.

 

5.     Do we share your data?

Your personal data will not be transmitted without your prior consent in addition to the cases explicitly mentioned in this data protection information. The data you provide by using the app will be passed on within the Austrian Red Cross for internal administrative purposes insofar as necessary. Your personal data may be transmitted in the following cases:

 

5.1 Abuse and law enforcement

If it is necessary to investigate any illegal or improper use of the app or for legal prosecution, personal data will be forwarded to law enforcement or other authorities and, if necessary, to damaged third parties or legal advisers. However, this shall only happen if there are any indications of illegal or abusive behavior; in the defense against such behavior is also our legitimate interest. Therefore, we rely on Art. 6 (1) (f) GDPR in conjunction with Art. 9 (2) (f) GDPR.

 

5.2 Epidemic Act

For the transmission of any information about specific cases of infection to the health authorities, Art. 9 (2) (i) GDPR in conjunction with § 10 (2, DPA a corresponding legal basis. The current epidemic shall be considered as a catastrophe in accordance with § 10 (1) GDPR. In addition, at the request of the district administrative authorities, the person responsible may be required to provide information on suspected cases and infections in accordance with § 5 (3) of the 1950 Epidemic Act.

 

We point out that, in accordance with Section 5(3) of the Epidemic Act, all persons, such as in particular doctors, laboratories, employers, family members and personnel of community institutions who could contribute to registrations, are obliged to provide information at the request of a district administration authority  (https://www.help.gv.at/Portal.Node/hlpd/public/content/behoerden.html).  Even if such a transfer of data (telephone number) by the Red Cross to the responsible district administration authority is not intended and is not technically implemented, we may be forced to hand over their telephone number. Should such a case arise, the legal basis is Art 6 sec. 1 lit c I combination with Art. 9 sec. 2 lit i GDPR.

 

If you have any questions about who shall be informed in case of any infections or suspected cases, please contact the health authorities. The “Symptom Checker” (questionnaire) reports a suspicion of the disease only to the Red Cross and not to authorities or other bodies.

5.4 Transfer to third countries

The ÖRK does not transfer personal data to the USA.

The development, operation and maintenance of the software was outsourced to Accenture GmbH, Stock Exchange building, Schottenring 16, 1010 Wien. As a processor, Accenture provides hosting and technical operation of the app and server, as well as maintenance in case of malfunction and the technical data security. As hosting service provider, Accenture uses Microsoft's Cloud Service called Azure. Within the scope of using the Azure Cloud, the customer can choose in which data centres or regions the data is to be stored or processed. When a resource is created, one chooses which data centre is to be used. In this case, the region EU West was chosen. Nevertheless, it cannot be completely ruled out that a transfer to the USA occurs, due to Microsoft's regular use of external service providers. In its judgement, the ECJ also examined the validity of the European Commission's decision 2010/87/EG on Standard Contractual Clauses ("SCC") and found that it was valid. Data transfers into the Microsoft Azure Cloud are currently based on Standard Contractual Clauses. However, the Court notes in particular that decision 2010/87/EG imposes on the data exporter and the recipient of the data (the "data importer") an obligation to verify, prior to each transfer and taking into account the circumstances of each transfer, whether that level of protection is met in the third country concerned, and that the data importer is required to inform the data exporter of the inability to comply with the standard data protection clauses and, if necessary, measures additional to those required by these clauses. The permissibility of transferring personal data to the USA on the basis of SCC depends on the outcome of the assessment in each individual case, taking into account the circumstances of the transfer and the additional measures taken.

Only the keys of those users who report an infection are deposited on the Azure Cloud. Once a day, each app retrieves the list of all keys of reported infections from the server. This list is then compared locally on the end device with the keys of a user's intensive contacts. In addition, the entire database server is operated in encrypted form.

In this context it should be noted, that due to the encryption(s) of the data, it is not possible for the processors to identify the data subjects. Such information cannot be derived from the processing context either. The data processed in the Azure Cloud is therefore not personal data for the processor. This (double) encryption serves as an additional security measure in the sense of the above mentioned ECJ jurisprudence and also as an additional security measure in accordance with the recommendations of the European Data Protection Board (Recommendations 01/2020 on measures that supplement transfer tools to ensure compliance with the EU level of protection of personal data).

 

5.5 Transfer to other App users

In connection with the use of the app, data is transmitted to individual app users, which is necessary for the app to function. The above mentioned pseudonymized random IDs of other app users are collected via the devices when a digital handshake is performed or suspicious cases, illnesses or all-clear are reported.

The legal basis for this is Art. 6 para. 1 lit. a and Art. 9 para. 2 lit. a GDPR (consent). However, we would like to point out that after revoking your consent (by deleting the Stopp Corona App), we have no possibility to delete random IDs directly from other devices, as we cannot assign these random IDs to a person or a specific device.

 

6.     When do we delete your data?

We delete or anonymize your personal data as soon as it is no longer required for the purposes for which we collected or used it in accordance with the above paragraphs. You can delete the data (random IDs) stored locally on your end device by uninstalling or deleting the app. Data on the device for the digital handshake with other devices are deleted from your device after 14 days.

As soon as a notification of sickness has been submitted, your data will be stored for 30 days after the notification. If it is necessary to clarify an illegal or abusive use of the app or for legal prosecution and if there are any specific indications of illegal or abusive behavior, your data will be stored for a period of up to three years after the notification of illness has been submitted.

Otherwise we will delete all data after the epidemic ends. Since an end is currently not foreseeable, no specific time of deletion can be provided. The Red Cross is part of the Austrian crisis unit and will make an appropriate and transparent decision in close consultation with the authorities and announce when the purpose of the application has been fulfilled. We all hope that this will happen as soon as possible and that we can make a contribution with the app.

 

7.     What rights do you have?

 

7.1 General remark on data subject rights

We are not able to identify you with the data processed within the Stopp Corona App, with the exception of your telephone number. Therefore, we cannot easily meet the requirements of articles 15 to 20 with regard to this data. In order to meet the requirements of articles 15 to 20, we will therefore generally need your cooperation.

With regard to data that enables us to identify you specifically (in any case your telephone number), we can fulfil the following rights of data subjects:

7.1 Right of access

You have the right to obtain from us upon request confirmation as to whether or not personal data concerning you are processed within the scope of Article 15 GDPR.

7.2 Right to rectify incorrect data

You have the right to request from us to immediately rectify your personal data if they are incorrect.

7.3 Right to deletion

You have the right to request the deletion of your personal data under the conditions described in Art. 17 GDPR. These requirements provide, in particular, a right to deletion if the personal data are no longer necessary for those purposes for which they were collected or otherwise processed as well as in cases of unlawful processing; even if you withdraw your consent and there is no other legal basis for processing, you have the right of erasure

7.4 Right to restriction of processing

You have the right to request that we restrict the processing pursuant to Art. 18 GDPR. This right exists in particular if the accuracy of the personal data between the user and us is controversial, for the duration required by the verification of the correctness and in case that the user requests a restricted processing instead of the deletion if there is an existing right to deletion; furthermore, in the event that the data are no longer required for the purposes pursued by us, but the user needs them to assert, exercise or defend legal claims.

7.5 Right to data portability

In principal, you have the right to receive from us the personal data relating to you that you have provided to us in a structured, common, machine-readable format pursuant to Art. 20 GDPR. Since we don’t have any personal data of yours on our servers, other that the telephone number if you have reported sick, this right practically will not be of high relevance.

7.6 Right to object

For all the data processing that we carry out on the basis of Art. 6 para. 1 letter f GDPR (see 5.3.1- 5.3.4), you have the right to object in accordance with Art. 21 para. 1 GDPR. This means that you can object to data processing for reasons arising from your particular situation at any time. However, an objection only leads to the omission of processing if the objection is justified by special reasons. We will no longer process the personal data unless we can prove compelling reasons for processing worthy of protection which outweigh your interests, rights and freedoms, or unless the processing serves to assert, exercise or defend legal claims. In this case, please send your objection to datenschutz@roteskreuz.at , contact us by phone at +43 1 58900-955 or by post to Austrian Red Cross, General Secretariat - Data Protection, Wiedner Hauptstrasse 32, 1040 Vienna.
